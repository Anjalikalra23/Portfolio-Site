Reflection on My Journey in the M.S. Applied Data Science Program

When I began my Master’s in Applied Data Science at Syracuse University, I entered with a strong desire to deepen my technical skills in machine learning, programming, and analytics. I expected the program to focus primarily on tools and algorithms. While it certainly delivered on that, what I gained went far beyond technical knowledge. The program reshaped how I think about data, problems, and impact.

Initially, I believed data science was mainly about building models and achieving high accuracy. However, as I progressed, I realized that true data science begins much earlier—at problem definition. I learned that asking the right question is often more important than choosing the right algorithm. This shift fundamentally changed my approach from coding-first to thinking-first.

Learning Outcomes and How I Achieved Them

Data Handling & Pipelines: Through coursework and my internship, I worked with large-scale datasets. I learned to clean, transform, and structure messy real-world data using Python and SQL. During my internship, I built pipelines to process healthcare data, which taught me how critical data quality is.

Full Data Science Lifecycle: I learned to move from raw data to actionable insights. This included EDA, modeling, and communication. My dashboards in Power BI helped stakeholders make decisions, showing me how analysis translates into real impact.

Machine Learning & Modeling: I worked with regression, classification, and deep learning. My CNN project on CIFAR-10 helped me understand feature extraction, tuning, and performance evaluation.

Visualization & Communication: I learned that insights must be clear and accessible. Through dashboards and presentations, I improved my ability to explain complex results to non-technical audiences.

Ethics in Data Science: Working with healthcare and biometric data made me aware of privacy and fairness issues. I learned that responsible AI is just as important as accurate AI.

Key Projects

1. FinChat AI: A multi-agent financial system using RAG and vector databases. This project taught me system design and how modern AI retrieves and processes information.

2. Multimodal Biometric System: Combined face, fingerprint, and iris recognition. This project strengthened my deep learning knowledge and raised ethical considerations.

3. CNN Image Classification: Built a deep learning model for CIFAR-10. This improved my understanding of neural networks and experimentation.

Internship Experience

My internship at the Central New York Health Home Network was one of the most impactful experiences. I worked with real healthcare data, built dashboards, and collaborated with stakeholders. This helped me bridge theory and practice and understand how data science is applied in real organizations.

Favorite Class

My favorite class was Human-Centered AI (HCAI). It changed how I think about AI—from purely technical systems to human-focused solutions. I learned about fairness, explainability, and usability. It taught me that AI should not just work—it should work for people.

Best Parts & Surprises

The best part of the program was working on real-world projects and seeing the impact of my work. One of the biggest surprises was how important communication is. Another was how messy real-world data can be compared to classroom datasets.

Conclusion

This program has been transformative. It strengthened my technical skills while also shaping my mindset. I learned how to think critically, communicate effectively, and build responsible data-driven solutions. I now feel prepared to step into the field as a confident and capable data scientist.
